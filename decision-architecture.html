Decision Architecture

The core of my work: a thesis on the structural design of decision-making, together with the software and instruments that make it executable.

The model runs

Organisational theory has a recurring weakness: it cannot be run. This one can. Fulcrum scores any organisation's structural health deterministically from 0 to 100: there is no randomness, so two people with the same model get the same number and every claim can be checked by anyone with the tool.

75.6 → 14.8

Typical organisation archetypes collapse as they grow.

98.7 → 66.4

Well-designed ones degrade gently under the same growth.

−9.40 vs +6.85

On the enterprise archetype, one approval-layer blunder outweighs the best available repair.

+9.7 vs +0.024

In a generated organisation of 1,732 teams, the median best local move dwarfs the best whole-organisation move. Value concentrates at the leaves.

The claims are falsifiable by design: model a realistic organisation in which adding an approval layer improves structural health and the framework is wrong in a way anyone can inspect.
